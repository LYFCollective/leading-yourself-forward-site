Identity Meets the Market

Your nine words told you who you are. Your ideal client told you who you are for. Your bridge reflection told you what happened when you showed up as that person. Session 4 is not about searching for a lane. It is about confirming the conclusion the data has already reached — and committing to it.

Session Outcome
You leave with your lane named and locked — not discovered, confirmed. Your first offer structure designed from what the lane requires energetically. A 90 day action plan with three specific commitments. And a 72 hour move that makes the lane real before Session 5.
01 Session Framework
The Principle
You Are Not Making a Decision. You Are Confirming a Conclusion.
The data from Sessions 2 and 3 has already pointed somewhere. Your nine words, your ideal client statement, and your bridge reflection are three signals. In most cases they all point in the same direction. The lane is not waiting to be found. It is waiting to be said out loud.
The Work
Depth in One Lane Produces What Breadth Never Can.
The practitioner who tries to serve everyone first builds nothing recognizable. No reputation, no referral network, no pricing power. The practitioner known for something specific gets recommended by name. Their rates reflect expertise, not availability. Every other lane opens to them from a position of proven depth.
The Commitment
Your Ideal Client Travels With You.
The lane you choose today is not a ceiling. It is the foundation from which every future stage is built. The person you described in Session 3 will find you in every lane you grow into. The magnet does not change. Only the stage gets bigger. Commit to the lane as an investment, not a sacrifice.
