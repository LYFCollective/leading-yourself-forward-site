Identity Meets the Market

In Session 2 you built the picture of who you are. Today you turn that picture outward and find the person who is already looking for exactly that. This is not a marketing exercise — it is a leadership decision.

Session Outcome
You leave with one specific person described — name, situation, problem, belief, and hope. Your positioning statement is held against that avatar and tested. The audience type where your magnet is strongest is identified. And you make one leadership commitment before Session 4 that puts that decision into the world.
01 Session Framework
The Principle
Specific Beats Broad. Every Time.
Most practitioners write for a general audience and reach no one deeply. The most decisive thing you can do as a leader is choose who you are for — not because everyone else is unworthy, but because a specific message spoken to one real person reaches every person who shares that situation.
The Work
Avatar Builds From Identity — Not Demographics.
Your nine words from Session 2 are the lens. The person you are building for emerges from who you actually are — not from a revenue opportunity, not from a market gap, not from what seems reasonable. Two participants go to the hot seat. The group watches the process before doing it themselves.
The Test
Would This Person Feel Found — Not Just Interested?
The matching moment asks one question: does your positioning statement land as an immediate yes for the specific person you just described? If the answer is not quite — examine the avatar first. The most common reason for a mismatch is that the person is still a type, not a human.
02 Your Session Forms
Form 01 · Before the Session
Pre-Work
Complete before Session 3. You audit who you have been building for, look for the pattern underneath your most aligned experiences, and imagine forward to who you are building toward. 20–25 minutes. The more specific and honest you are, the more powerful the session will be.
Due before Session 3
Open Form
Form 02 · During the Session
Session Worksheet
Your in-session working document. Client audit, ideal client avatar build (Parts 1–2), the matching moment against your positioning statement, and the audience fit assessment. You work through this individually after the hot seat demonstrations, then the group discusses.
Completed during Session 3
Open Form
Form 03 · After the Session
Post-Session Commitment
Locks your avatar decision and converts it into one specific action before Session 4. Your nine words, your final avatar description, your positioning statement match, your strongest audience type, and the one thing you will change, add, or stop — with a deadline.
Due within 24 hours of Session 3
Open Form
Form 04 · Between Sessions
72-Hour Bridge
One signal. Put your ideal client description and positioning statement into the world once — in 72 hours — before Session 4. A post, a conversation, an updated bio, an offer framed for the person you described. The reflection you bring back opens Session 4's lane decision.
